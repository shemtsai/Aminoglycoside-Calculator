Aminoglycoside Guidance
CPOV Guidance
  • iVent: Open 'Communication to Clinical Specialist' for any aminoglycoside ordered for continuation >48 hours
  • iVent should notify clinical specialist on aminoglycoside order so they can appropriately follow-up on duration, monitoring, and need for pharmacy consult
Exclusion Criteria
  • One-time doses (e.g., double coverage, UTI) do not require maintenance dosing orders
  • Intra-op dosing including surgical prophylaxis do not require maintenance dosing orders or modifications unless ordered for >48 hours and impaired renal function
  • For treatment of chorioamnionitis, endometritis, or post-abortal infection, continue dosing per the order set (e.g., gentamicin 5 mg/kg IV every 24 hours, unless patient has significant renal impairment, then follow below dosing).

Gentamicin/Tobramycin (High-dose Extended-Interval)

Initial Dose:
  • 7 mg/kg IV x1
Maintenance Interval:
  • CrCl ≥60 mL/min: Every 24 hours
  • CrCl 40–59 mL/min: Every 36 hours
  • CrCl 20–39 mL/min: Every 48 hours
  • CrCl <20 mL/min: Give initial dose, then order gentamicin/tobramycin placeholder

Maintenance Interval:

  • CrCl 40–59 mL/min: Every 36 hours
  • CrCl 20–39 mL/min: Every 48 hours
  • CrCl <20 mL/min: Give initial dose, then order amikacin placeholder

When to use: If a patient is ordered for an aminoglycoside with an indication "TB/mycobacterial infections" or if clinical notes suggest aminoglycoside is used for treatment of mycobacterial infection. Below guidance is only for systemic aminoglycosides.

Continuation of Home Therapy (NTM)

  • Verify and continue home aminoglycoside regimen (usually amikacin, less often tobramycin)
  • If home dose regimen is unknown, patient is coming from outside facility where they received aminoglycosides, and/or patient comes in with acute kidney injury from baseline, please obtain the respective aminoglycoside serum concentration in the AM, order a placeholder with admin instructions "pending gentamicin/tobramycin/amikacin concentration, clinical pharmacist to dose during daytime", then open an i-vent for clinical specialist to follow-up on
Typical Amikacin Dosing Patterns (for reference)
  • 15–25 mg/kg IV three times weekly (preferred)
  • 10–15 mg/kg IV five days per week or daily

New Start (TB / Mycobacterial Infections)

Treatment initiation (off-hours/weekend)
  • If urgent initiation is required, it is reasonable to start amikacin 15 mg/kg IV three times weekly for patients not on HD, regardless of renal function
  • In addition to 'Communication to Clinical Specialist', reach out to ID pharmacist to notify for reassessment of regimen in daytime/weekday

Gentamicin (Gram-Positive Synergy – Endocarditis)

  • Indication: Gram-positive synergy (e.g., enterococcal or staphylococcal endocarditis)
Dose
  • 1 mg/kg IV every 8 hours
Maintenance Interval (renal adjustment)
  • CrCl ≥60 mL/min: Every 8 hours
  • CrCl 40–59 mL/min: Every 12 hours
  • CrCl 20–39 mL/min: Every 24 hours
  • CrCl <20 mL/min: Give initial dose, then order gentamicin placeholder
Aminoglycoside Dosing Weight Selection
  • Primary approach: Use Ideal Body Weight (IBW)
  • Obesity: If Actual Body Weight (ABW) ≥ 1.25 × IBW → use Adjusted Body Weight (AdjBW)
  • Underweight: If ABW < IBW → use ABW (Actual Body Weight)
  • Critically ill: ABW may be used based on clinical judgment
IBW (Ideal Body Weight)
  • Male: 50 + 2.3 kg per inch over 60 in
  • Female: 45 + 2.3 kg per inch over 60 in
AdjBW (Adjusted Body Weight)
  • IBW + 0.4 × (ABW − IBW)
Abbreviations
  • ABW: Actual Body Weight
  • IBW: Ideal Body Weight
  • AdjBW: Adjusted Body Weight
When to Use Conventional vs High-Dose Extended Interval dosing
Default: High-dose extended interval is preferred for the vast majority of patients and is reflected in the all tabs except for the synergy tab. Conventional dosing (also known as traditional) should only be used when there is a clearly documented indication for gram-positive synergy.
Default Strategy: High-Dose Extended Interval (Preferred)
  • Use for: All empiric or definitive gram-negative infections
  • Rationale: Maximizes peak/MIC and exploits post-antibiotic effect
  • Benefits: Lower nephrotoxicity risk via drug-free interval
  • Monitoring: Extended-interval nomogram or random level strategy
  • Clinical scenarios: sepsis, bacteremia, pneumonia, complicated intra-abdominal infection, UTI, etc.
Conventional Dosing (also known as traditional dosing)
  • Use ONLY when: Documented gram-positive synergy indication exists
  • Primary indication: Endocarditis (e.g., enterococcal or staphylococcal synergy regimens)
  • Rationale: Requires sustained low concentrations rather than high peaks
  • Monitoring: Peak and trough levels required
  • Avoid use in: routine gram-negative infections (inferior PK/PD exposure)
